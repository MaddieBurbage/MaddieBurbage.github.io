Craft It

(November/December 2017)

A dynamic community-based crafting website. The Repository.

Purpose

I have fond memories of learning to knit scarves with my aunt and sawing blocks of wood into a toy train with my dad. They're both excellent craftspeople and it shows in their creations and their ease with teaching. What I did miss growing up was an easy way to learn how to make things that my family wasn't able to teach. If a crafting idea didn't start with a food I could find on AllRecipes I would have to scroll through endless blog results on Google to find a starting point for my project. I designed CraftIt to be a solution to my problem, a site where users could easily create and share every type of craft with the world. Most importantly, searchers hoping to find the perfect inspiration could easily narrow down crafts until they found something that worked for them.

User Interface

To make crafting tutorials relevant to wannabe-creators, I had some important info to gather. For starters, logistics like time taken, materials used, and style of craft would be very important to anyone searching for the perfect project. My design for the project creation page was a careful balance between info necessary for searchers to have a successful time on the site and creators adding projects with ease.

Data Storage

Beyond initial project creation, I wanted to make user feedback and interaction easy so that each user would have a successful time with the site. Features like rating projects, reviewing and sharing photos with other users, and saving favorite projects were all useful tools to shape the search and recommendation processes on CraftIt. Of course, this on top of project and user data adds up to a lot of data storage and retrieval. Through a combination of PHP and JavaScript with Ajax, I made the site respond fluidly to user input, whether in the login, search, or review process. This was also my first rigorous introduction to SQL, which powered databases containing the bulk of the site, as the true depth of the site lies in user-inputted data rather than any pages I might make.
