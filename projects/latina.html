Latina Lingua

(April/May 2017)

A Latin-language-learning program for Windows. The Repository.

Purpose

The trickiest part of picking up the Latin language is its very different style of grammar. Unlike English, the order of words in a sentence really doesn't matter, and writers need to add endings to words to tell readers what words mean. The system gives a lot of flexibility to poets but requires a lot of memorization for learners. For instance, every noun has 12 forms, adjectives can have 36 forms, and verbs have upwards of 250 forms.

Design

Most other languages have ample tools available to learn their vocabulary and forms, but Latin is, well, dead. So Latina is pretty unique as a tool to teach word forms. Users can hone in on problem areas by selecting which types of words to drill and through clever grading they can try to fix their mistakes before seeing the corrected answers. Flexible dictionaries mean that users can import whichever words they are learning at the moment to get practice that fits their education. Right now Latina is completed for nouns and adjectives but I could see extending it for verbs and further parts of speech later.

Grammar

The largest component of the project was to program a customizable but accurate grammar-checker. Beyond finding all the patterns in word forms, I searched for similarities in patterns across words so the space requirements of the program wouldn't explode as I added more features.
